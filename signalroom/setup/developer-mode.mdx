# iPhone settings

Status: Current iPhone-side checklist. This page covers every iOS toggle that affects whether a lane runs cleanly; pages on the runner install and lane readiness pick up from here.

Run through this **once per iPhone, in order, before installing the on-device runner**. Two of these, Light appearance and StandBy off, are the most common cause of failed sessions in newly-set-up lanes.

## 1. Developer Mode

**Settings → Privacy & Security → Developer Mode → On.**

The iPhone will prompt to restart. Confirm. After reboot, confirm Developer Mode is enabled again at the second prompt.

Required for iOS 17+. Without Developer Mode, the on-device runner cannot be installed.

**If Developer Mode is missing from Privacy & Security:** the option only appears after the iPhone has been connected to a Mac running Xcode (or an Apple-signed equivalent) at least once. Connect to the Mac host, then re-check.

## 2. Trust the Mac

Plug the iPhone into the Mac host. When iOS shows **"Trust This Computer?"**, tap **Trust** and enter the passcode.

iOS remembers this trust permanently for the specific Mac. If you replace the Mac, you'll need to re-trust on each device.

## 3. Auto-Lock = Never

**Settings → Display & Brightness → Auto-Lock → Never.**

If "Never" makes you uncomfortable, the minimum acceptable value is 5 minutes, anything shorter will interrupt longer in-app flows.

**Why this matters:** auto-lock cuts off whatever the iPhone is doing, including an active upload. The session breaks, the video doesn't publish, and the account is left in an uncertain state.

## 4. Brightness ≥ 50%

**Settings → Display & Brightness**: slider at the middle or higher.

**Why this matters:** Warmr recognizes TikTok screen elements visually. Low brightness reduces on-screen contrast and slows recognition, which slows the whole session.

## 5. Appearance = Light (required)

**Settings → Display & Brightness → Appearance → Light.**

Required. On Dark appearance, TikTok's pre-publish screen renders almost entirely in near-black, and the on-device runner cannot reliably locate the on-screen targets it needs to tap, uploads simply do not complete.

**If you prefer Dark elsewhere on your phone**, set it specifically to **Light** on the iPhones used with Warmr. Do **not** use "Automatic", it switches at night and breaks scheduled sessions.

## 6. Disable iCloud Photos

**Settings → Photos → iCloud Photos → Off.**

Keeps the iPhone's gallery limited to the content you prepared for publishing. Otherwise foreign photos from your iCloud library can sync down and accidentally end up in a post.

## 7. StandBy = Off (required)

**Settings → StandBy → Off.**

Required. StandBy is the iOS 17+ feature that turns a charging iPhone into a desk clock when it's lying on its side. It activates after about 30 seconds when the device is *charging* + *horizontal* + *locked* + *idle*.

**Once StandBy is active, iOS blocks programmatic app launches.** Warmr cannot start TikTok, and the session stalls. Symptoms: the session sits idle for several minutes, then the device shows up as `standby` (or simply stops progressing) in the dashboard.

**Alternative (less reliable):** always keep the iPhone *vertical* while charging. StandBy needs the phone lying flat to activate, so a stand that holds it upright works. **Portrait Orientation Lock in Control Center does not help**: StandBy reads the accelerometer directly.

Toggling StandBy off doesn't affect anything else on the phone.

## 8. Reduce Motion (optional)

**Settings → Accessibility → Motion → Reduce Motion → On.**

Cuts iOS animations, which makes screen transitions slightly faster across the whole device. Not required, but recommended once the rest of the lane is working.

## 9. TikTok app

- **Install TikTok** from the App Store on the iPhone.
- **Open it at least once**, accept the initial prompts, sign in to the first account you intend to use.
- **Language:** English. Other languages may work but are not regularly tested.

The runner will not install TikTok for you. If TikTok is missing from the device, posting runs will fail with a "TikTok not installed" error.

## Per-iPhone checklist

Before installing the on-device runner, confirm:

- [ ] Developer Mode = On
- [ ] "Trust This Computer" confirmed on the Mac you'll use
- [ ] Auto-Lock = Never (or ≥ 5 minutes)
- [ ] Brightness ≥ 50%
- [ ] Appearance = **Light** (not Dark, not Automatic)
- [ ] iCloud Photos = Off
- [ ] **StandBy = Off**
- [ ] TikTok installed, opened at least once, signed into the first account
- [ ] At least 5 GB free (15 GB+ preferred)

All boxes ticked? Continue to [Trust and runner setup](/signalroom/setup/trust-runner) to install the on-device runner.
